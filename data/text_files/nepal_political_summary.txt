NEPAL'S POLITICAL LANDSCAPE: A HISTORIC TRANSFORMATION (2025–2026)
====================================================================

1. THE 2025 GEN Z UPRISING
---------------------------
By mid-2025, public frustration in Nepal had peaked over rampant corruption, high youth
unemployment, and the perceived nepotism of the political elite — often criticized through
the viral "#NepoKid" and "#NepoBabies" social media trends. The immediate catalyst for
the unrest was the government's decision on 4 September 2025 to ban 26 social media
platforms, including YouTube, Facebook, and WhatsApp.

The protests were Gen Z-led and deadly — at least 77 people were killed — and ultimately
toppled the government. After the incumbent Oli government resigned in the wake of the
September protests, the president dissolved Parliament and tasked an interim government,
led by former chief justice Sushila Karki, with holding snap elections in March 2026.


2. THE MARCH 2026 GENERAL ELECTION
-------------------------------------
Early general elections were held on 5 March 2026 to elect the 275 members of the House
of Representatives. The number of registered voters rose by nearly one million since the
2022 election, as the youth-led uprising generated greater interest in politics.

A total of 320,000 security personnel from the Nepal Police, Armed Police Force, and Nepal
Army were deployed to maintain law and order during the elections.

The result was a political earthquake. The Rastriya Swatantra Party (RSP), with Balen Shah
as their candidate for prime minister, won a landslide victory — gaining a majority in the
House of Representatives without the need for a coalition, the first time this had happened
since 1999.


3. BALEN SHAH: NEPAL'S NEW PRIME MINISTER
------------------------------------------
Balendra Shah — popularly known as Balen — is a politician, structural engineer, and rapper
who has been serving as the Prime Minister of Nepal since 2026. At age 36, he is the world's
youngest serving state leader.

Shah won over the Nepali youth vote with his outsized social media presence, with over
1.2 million Instagram followers and 3.8 million Facebook followers. He secured 68,348 votes
in the Jhapa-5 constituency — the highest vote total ever recorded in Nepal's parliamentary
election history — defeating four-time former Prime Minister K.P. Sharma Oli by a margin of
49,614 votes.

At 12:34 pm on March 27, 2026 — an auspicious time chosen through astrology — Shah was
sworn in as the country's youngest-ever elected prime minister, appointing a 14-member
cabinet whose oldest member is aged 51.


4. EARLY ACTIONS OF THE NEW GOVERNMENT
-----------------------------------------
The new administration wasted no time in signaling a break from the past:

- By the morning after the swearing-in, the Shah government authorized the arrests of
  former PM Oli and former home minister Ramesh Lekhak on charges of criminal negligence
  related to the September protests, and released a 100-point governance reform agenda.

- The next morning, authorities arrested another former minister on money laundering
  charges and opened corruption investigations into three former prime ministers.

- On 20 April 2026, the government announced a reform to switch government employee
  salaries from a monthly to a fortnightly (15-day) payment system.


5. WHAT THIS MEANS FOR NEPAL
------------------------------
Nepal has seen 14 governments and nine prime ministers since 2008, with power rotating
between former Maoist rebels, the Communist Party of Nepal (UML), and the Nepali Congress.
The RSP's sweeping majority marks a decisive break from this cycle.

Shah is also the first Prime Minister of Nepal from the Madheshi community, signaling a
shift in the country's historically hill-caste-dominated politics.

Nepal is in the midst of one of the most dramatic political transformations in its modern
history, driven largely by its youth.
